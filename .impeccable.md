## Design Context

### Users

LightPick serves three overlapping creator archetypes who share an "idea-first" mindset:

- **Indie content creators / solo founders** — producing content for YouTube, TikTok, or marketing. Want speed, sane defaults, and to ship without becoming a video editor. Time-poor, taste-rich.
- **Pro creators (filmmakers, motion designers)** — skilled video professionals using AI to accelerate craft, not replace it. Expect power, density, keyboard control, and respect for their existing mental models (timeline, composition, color).
- **Storytellers / writers exploring video** — people who think in scripts, beats, and emotional arcs rather than timelines. The chat / Milkdown editor is their primary surface; the node canvas is downstream of ideas.

All three are using LightPick as a **co-creation canvas**: they bring intent, agents do execution, and the interface is the negotiation surface between the two. They are using it on desktop in long, focused sessions — not on the go.

### Brand Personality

**Three words: alive, intentional, generous.**

- **Alive** — the canvas reacts. Nodes settle, agents move, generated content arrives with presence. Not "fun" in a toy way — alive like a studio with people working in it.
- **Intentional** — every choice (color, motion, copy) is a decision a designer made. No defaults visible. No "just because the framework gave us this."
- **Generous** — gives the user room. Generous whitespace, generous typography, generous time on entrances. Doesn't crowd.

Voice and tone: **confident creative partner, not assistant**. Speaks plainly, never apologizes, never over-explains. Microcopy has personality but doesn't crack jokes. Uses active voice. Treats the user as a peer who knows what they want.

Emotional goals, in priority order:
1. **Trust** — agents are doing real work, the user must feel they're in control of it.
2. **Momentum** — every interaction should feel like progress; never dead time.
3. **Delight** — earned through craft (motion, type, polish), not through stickers/confetti.

### Aesthetic Direction

**Light-first, dark supported.** Light is the canonical mode. Dark must be polished but is secondary — matches the current `globals.css` default and the "creative writing tool" feel more than the "DAW" feel.

**Visual tone:** modern, restrained, slightly editorial. The brand is warm coral (`#FF6B50`) over neutral slates — confident accent color, not a palette. Type pairing is **Space Grotesk display + Inter body + JetBrains Mono for system surfaces** (already wired in `app/layout.tsx`). Letter-spacing on headings is tight (`-0.02em`); body breathes.

**Anti-references — what LightPick must never look like:**
- **Generic AI SaaS landing pages.** No purple-to-blue gradients. No glassmorphism cards as decoration. No hero metric layouts (huge number + small label + sparkline). No cyan-on-dark "neural" aesthetic. No gradient text on headings. No same-sized card grids of features-with-icons. The "made by ChatGPT" tells from 2024–2025 are explicit anti-patterns.

**References for the right feel** (directional, not literal):
- **Linear** — calm density, considered motion, restraint as a brand value.
- **Things 3** — generous whitespace, soft warmth, every detail intentional.
- **Arc Browser / Raycast** — playfulness through motion and personality, never through visual noise.
- **Figma's canvas** — a working surface that gets out of the way.

### Design Principles

1. **Canvas first, chrome last.** The user's work — the canvas, the document, the generated image — is the hero. Every panel, button, or label that frames it must justify its pixel weight. When in doubt, remove the frame.

2. **Motion is communication, not decoration.** Use motion to convey state changes (a node arriving, an agent thinking, content ready). Use exponential easing — never bounce or elastic. One well-orchestrated transition beats five micro-interactions. Default duration short (150–250ms); reserve longer (400–600ms) for entrances that deserve weight.

3. **Coral is a verb.** The brand color marks action and life — selection, focus, generation in progress, the agent's voice. It must never be wallpaper. Gray-on-coral text is forbidden; tint into the coral family instead. Other category colors (image/video/audio/custom) are semantic, not decorative.

4. **Type does the heavy lifting.** Hierarchy comes from type scale, weight, and tracking — not from boxes, borders, or background fills. Prefer flattening cards over nesting them. A heading + a thin rule beats a card with shadow nine times out of ten.

5. **Trust through transparency.** When an agent is working, the user must see *what* and *why* — not a generic spinner. When generation fails, say what failed and what to try, never "Something went wrong." Surfacing state honestly is part of the brand promise (Anti-Slop = anti-opacity).
